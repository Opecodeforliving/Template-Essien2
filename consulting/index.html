    IT Enterprise Risk and Compliance Management

    Enterprise risk management (ERM) is a structured and disciplined approach to managing risk. ERM aligns the organization's strategies, processes, technology and people with the purpose of improving its ability to evaluate and manage the uncertainties it faces as it creates value. An enterprise-wide risk management capability increases the risk-sensitivity of the organisation and reduces the inevitable functional, departmental and cultural barriers that exist in most organizations. ERM is an integrated, forward-looking and process-orientated approach to managing all key business risks and opportunities - not just financial ones - with the intent of maximizing value for the enterprise as a whole. Our IT related business objectives focused approach helps our clients understand their risk portfolio and achieve an outcome that exploits their full potential. This includes a review of effectiveness of existing risk management processes, gap analysis, strategy planning and implementation, reporting to stakeholders and ongoing assessment.

    Business Resilience

    Beyond protection of information asset, an objection of information assurance is continual availability of data. Disaster Recovery (DR) and Business Continuity Plans (BCP) helps achieve availability in support of confidentiality and integrity of information asset by implementing simple, maintainable, and auditable BCP/DR Plans. DR/BCP plans will outline the courses of action to be taken in the event of an IT or Business emergency and the process for each business unit/department to follow in their recovery to normal business operations. Since all disasters tend to be unique, these DR and BC Plans will not address any single type of disaster scenario, nor define a specific recovery strategy for all possibilities. We rather work with you, understand your business units and proffer plans that uniquely suit your environment and align to your business requirement.
